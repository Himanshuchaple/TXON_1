Steve Jobs

Innovator. Visionary. Genius.

Biography

Steven Paul Jobs was an American entrepreneur and business magnate. He was the chairman, chief executive officer (CEO), and co-founder of Apple Inc.; chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar; a member of The Walt Disney Company's board of directors following its acquisition of Pixar; and the founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT. Jobs is widely recognized as a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.

Jobs was born in San Francisco, California, and put up for adoption. He was raised in the San Francisco Bay Area. He attended Reed College in 1972 before dropping out that same year, and traveled through India in 1974 seeking enlightenment and studying Zen Buddhism. His declassified FBI report states that he used marijuana and LSD while he was in college, and once told a reporter that taking LSD was "one of the two or three most important things" he did in his life.

Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, at his home in Palo Alto, California, due to complications from pancreatic cancer.

Timeline

  • 1955

    Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco, California.

  • 1976

    Steve Jobs co-founds Apple Computer Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne.

  • 1985

    Steve Jobs resigns from Apple after a power struggle with John Sculley.

  • 1986

    Steve Jobs acquires The Graphics Group, which is later renamed Pixar.

  • 1996

    Steve Jobs returns to Apple as interim CEO after the company's financial struggles.

  • 2011

    Steve Jobs dies at the age of 56 from complications of pancreatic cancer.
